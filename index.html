All you need to know about the worlds top Ski Resorts

Whistler

Switzerland |

Key Facts

  • The nearest airport is Vancouver which is an hour and a half drive away.
  • The elevation is 2214ft.
  • It has 37 lifts.
  • It has an hourly lift capacity of 70,000 skiers/boarders.
  • It is made of 2 mountains. Whistler and Blackcomb.
  • 200+ trails.
  • 11.7m average snowfall

Things to know

Whistler opened in January 1966 as part of a bid to win the 1968 Winter Olympics. Whistler is consistently ranked the No. 1 ski resort in North America with over 8,171 acres of terrain. As a true four season resort, Whistler Blackcomb offers skiing and snowboarding in the winter and downhill mountain biking and alpine hiking in the summer. Guests can even enjoy summer skiing/riding on the Horstman Glacier in June and July. At Whistler Blackcomb, there is something for everyone all year long.

    Verbier

    Switzerland |

    Key Facts

    • The nearest airport is Sion which is 45 minutes drive away.
    • The summit is at 12,000ft.
    • 400 acres of skiable terrain.
    • It has 82 lifts.
    • Verbier has 10m of snowfall a year.
    • Part of the 4 valleys ski area.
    • Population of 35,000 in season.

    Things to know

    The luxurious Swiss village of Verbier has long been associated with the rich and famous. Its understated style and ridiculously amazing skiing has placed it far above much of the rest of the competition, and for good reason. Everything you need to know The wealth of the clientele is immediately apparent; a chalet can set you back at an average of a cool £8 million and the average pint of beer will set you back around £6. Whilst other ski resorts might be great at some things, Verbier has it all: royals, celebrities, restaurants, après ski and some of the best off-piste skiing in Europe – that is if you can get out of bed in the morning.

    St Anton

    Austria |

    Key Facts

    • The nearest Airport is Innsbruck which is 100km away
    • The Ski season runs from december to april
    • Highest altitude of 2811m
    • 22km squared approx. skiing area.
    • 6.10m of snowfall a year.
    • 87 lifts
    • The lifts take 57000 skiers per hour

    Things to know

    St Anton is one of the world's best resorts for competent skiers and riders, particularly those with the energy to après-ski as hard as they ski. If you want to, you can party from 3pm to 3am. The resort is a long, sprawling place, almost a town rather than a village, squeezed into a narrow valley.St Anton has always been a great skiing resort, not to mention home to the best après-ski in the Alps. The ski area itself, known as Ski Arlberg, incorporates the neighbouring resorts of St Christoph, Zurs and Lech and is now linked with a modern, efficient lift system meaning that it now possible to ski to all these areas.

    Val d'Isere

    France |

    Key Facts

    • The closest airport is Chambery which is 134km away.
    • The season runs from November - May
    • The highest altitude is 3656m.
    • 300km of skiable pistes.
    • 79 lifts.
    • 320 snow cannons.
    • The longest run is 10km

    Things to know

    Val d'Isere, in the Savoie area of France, is a colourful winter sports haven that began as a farm community in the years BC. It is the better-known part of Espace Killy. The huge skiing domain, shares itself with purpose-built Tignes. The area is named for Olympic hero Jean-Claude Killy, who grew up there. Val d’Isère has a reputation for some of the liveliest nightlife in the Alps and there are dozens and dozens of bars and night spots to choose from. Everything from a casual beer to the very best champagne, Val d’Isere has great après options for everyone
